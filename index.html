Do You Believe?

Turns Out Many Do!

Andrew Hott

Jun 7, 2025

I've always kept an open mind when it comes to various phenomena, but nothing's ever been quite as interesting as UFOs and aliens to me. The allure of the unknown has always captivated human imagination, and the mysteries surrounding unidentified flying objects (UFOs) OR unidentified airial phenomena (UAP) and extraterrestrial life continue to spark curiosity and debate. Recent surveys reveal that a significant portion of the American public shares this intrigue. A poll conducted by Decision Desk HQ and NewsNation found that 44% of Americans believe the government is concealing information about UFOs, while 28% disagree, and another 28% are unsure.

This skepticism isn't new. Earlier studies have shown that a majority of Americans suspect the government is withholding information about UFOs. Despite official statements asserting transparency, many remain unconvinced, pointing to historical instances where information was either classified or misrepresented. The persistence of these beliefs raises important questions about transparency, trust, and the nature of the information being withheld. Are we truly being kept in the dark, or is there a more complex explanation behind the government's reticence?

Whistleblowers Speak Out

Andrew Hott

Jun 7, 2025

In recent years, a growing number of whistleblowers have come forward with astonishing and often controversial claims regarding secretive government programs tied to UFOs and potential extraterrestrial contact. These individuals, many of whom have served in highly sensitive military, intelligence, or defense positions, allege that behind closed doors, the government has been conducting investigations into unidentified aerial phenomena (UAPs). According to their testimonies, not only has the government documented and tracked these unusual craft, but it has also recovered physical materials, sometimes even entire vehicles that defy known technological capabilities. Some go further, suggesting that reverse engineering efforts have been underway for decades, potentially involving technology not of human origin. These bold assertions have sparked widespread debate and increased pressure for greater transparency on the issue of UAPs.

One of the most prominent and widely discussed figures in this movement is David Grusch, a former U.S. intelligence officer who gained national attention in 2023 after testifying before Congress. Grusch alleged that the U.S. government is in possession of "non-human biologics" as well as several crashed or intact unidentified craft of non-human origin. Although his statements lack public physical evidence, supporters point to his security clearance and extensive intelligence background as indicators of credibility. His willingness to testify under oath and speak openly about what he described as deeply classified programs has further elevated the seriousness of his claims. Grusch's testimony has fueled both renewed media interest and calls for formal investigations, contributing to cultural shift toward questioning how much we know, as well as a greater acceptance for those engrossed by this topic.
